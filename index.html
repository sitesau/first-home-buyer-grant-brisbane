First Home Buyer Grant Brisbane Explained

Brisbane buyers researching first home support often lump three separate mechanisms together: the Queensland First Home Owner Grant, a federal low-deposit guarantee scheme, and a state stamp duty concession. Each has its own eligibility test, and a buyer can potentially qualify for more than one at once, or for none, depending on the property and their circumstances.

A dedicated guide to first home buyer loans in Brisbane covers how these pathways interact with loan structuring in more depth. This page focuses purely on the grant and scheme side, so treat it as a starting point rather than a substitute for that fuller explanation or for advice from a broker who can check current criteria against your situation.

What the Queensland grant is generally for

The Queensland First Home Owner Grant exists to help eligible buyers into a new home, and it sits alongside (not instead of) any low-deposit or lending arrangement a buyer sets up separately. Because grant rules, price caps and property-type conditions are set and periodically revised by the state government, this page deliberately avoids quoting a specific dollar figure or cap. Confirm the live figure directly with Queensland Revenue Office or with a broker who checks it as part of every application.

  • Generally aimed at new or substantially renovated homes rather than established dwellings, though conditions vary by scheme version
  • Sits alongside, not instead of, your home loan application
  • Time-limited in most iterations, so timing a purchase against the current rules matters

Low-deposit pathways and lenders mortgage insurance

Separate from the state grant, some first home buyers can access a low-deposit purchase pathway that reduces or removes the need for lenders mortgage insurance, an added cost that otherwise applies when a deposit sits below a lender's standard threshold. One example scenario reported by brokers in this network involved a young couple purchasing in inner Brisbane on a limited deposit, where a specialist first home buyer broker identified grant eligibility and a low-deposit option that allowed the purchase to proceed with a 5% deposit and no lenders mortgage insurance, a meaningful saving on upfront costs for that household.

That outcome depended on the buyer's income, the property, and the specific scheme rules at the time, so it illustrates what is possible rather than a guaranteed result for every applicant.

Stamp duty concessions for first home buyers

Queensland also offers a transfer (stamp) duty concession for eligible first home buyers, which can meaningfully reduce settlement costs on top of any grant. As with the grant itself, price thresholds and eligibility rules are set by the state and reviewed periodically, so this guide points to the general mechanism rather than a specific dollar cutoff. A mortgage broker preparing your finance application will typically flag whether a concession is likely to apply based on the purchase price and property type you are considering.

Because a mortgage broker is paid by the lender on settlement rather than by the buyer, getting this checked as part of a loan enquiry generally carries no separate cost to the buyer.

Who this guide applies to

This information is most relevant to Brisbane buyers who have not previously owned a home in Australia and are weighing up a purchase in the CBD, inner suburbs, or growth corridors on the north, south or western fringes of the city. It is less relevant to established investors, or to buyers who have owned property previously and are not eligible for first home buyer schemes, since grant and concession rules are specifically restricted to first-time buyers.

  • Buyers comparing a new build against an established home, where grant eligibility can differ
  • Buyers with a deposit below the standard threshold, who may benefit from a low-deposit pathway

Common questions

Can I get the Queensland grant and a stamp duty concession at the same time? Potentially, since they are separate mechanisms with their own eligibility tests, but both are restricted to first home buyers and depend on the property and purchase price. Confirm both directly with Queensland Revenue Office or a broker before relying on either.

Does a low-deposit pathway replace the need for the state grant? No. A low-deposit pathway addresses the deposit and lenders mortgage insurance side of a purchase, while the state grant is a separate payment. Buyers can be eligible for one, both, or neither depending on their situation.

Who checks whether I am eligible for these schemes? A mortgage broker typically checks eligibility as part of preparing a loan application, alongside your own research directly with Queensland Revenue Office and ASIC Moneysmart.
